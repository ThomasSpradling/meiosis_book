Glossary

Anaphase I: The third step of meiosis I where the chromosomes move apart.

Anaphase II: The third step of meiosis I where the chromatids move apart.

Cell: The smallest living unit of our body. We have trillions of them!

Centriole: Small bodies inside the a cell that manuevers spindle fibers.

Centromere: The central area of a chromosome.

Centrosome: The area of the cell that has spindle fibers and centrioles.

Chiasma: The portion where chromosomes cross over.

Chromatid: A major piece of a chromosome. Chromosomes may each have either one or two.

Chromatin: Packed down DNA that is sprawled everywhere in a cell's nucleus.

Chromosome: Packed down DNA that is packaged into chromatids and held by a centromere.

Crossing Over: The process where two chromosomes exchange genes.

Diploid: A cell with a full set of chromosomes. This number is 46 for human cells.

Egg Cell: The kind of gamete that come from females.

Fermentation: The process of combining a sperm cell and egg cell into a zygote.

Gamete: The end-product haploid cells of meiosis.

Gametocyte: The cells capable of meiosis. They will turn into gametes.

Gene: A portion of DNA that has a trait, like nose shape.

Haploid: A cell with a half set of chromosomes. This number is 23 in humans.

Homologous: Two chromosomes have genes in the same locations. They do not have to have to same exact values of genes though. One chromosome may have a gene that is "eye color" = brown, while another "eye color" = blue.

Independent Assortment: The fact that it doesn't matter which side a chromosome is in metaphase I.

Interphase: The step before meiosis where the cell prepares and duplicates chromosomes (DNA).

Karyotype: The list of all chromosomes in a cell. In humans there are 23 different pairs.

Locus: The portion of a chromosome where there is a gene.

Meiosis: The cell divion process (into four cells) of gametocytes that allows variation in us!

Meiosis I: The first division of meiosis.

Meiosis II: The second division of meiosis.

Metaphase I: The process where chromosomes align in pairs in the middle of the cell during meiosis I.

Metaphase II: The process where chromosomes align in the middle of the cell during meiosis II.

Mitosis: The process where a cell divides into two identical cells.

Nucleus: The part of the cell that contains all the DNA.

Oocyte: A gametocyte found in females.

Polar Body: One of two resulting gametes during a oocyte's meiosis.

Prophase I: The first phase of meiosis I where the chromosomes cross over and the nucleus dissolves.

Prophase II: The first phase of meiosis II where the nucleus dissolves.

Random Fertilization: The fact that a random sperm cell will fertilize with a random egg cell.

Somatic Cell: A regular cell of our body; one that doesn't go through meiosis.

Sperm Cell: A gamete cell that is the product of a spermatocyte's meiosis.

Spermatocyte: A gametocyte males have.

Spindle Fiber: The thin tube that are responsible for movement of chromosomes (DNA).

Telophase I: The fourth step of meiosis I. It is the step where a cell begins to divide.

Telophase II: The fourth step of meiosis II. It is the step where a cell begins to divide for its second time.

Tetrad: A row of two homologous bichromatid (two chromatids) chromosomes.

Zygote: The product of a sperm cell combining with an egg cell by fertilization. This is the single cell that is responsible for the making of all our body's cells.
